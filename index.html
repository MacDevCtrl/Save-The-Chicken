What NOT to feed your dog

By Sarah Bones | June 21, 2026

Hey dog lovers! Today we need to have a very serious conversation about dog food. We all love our furry friends, but sometimes we forget that they are dogs, and not tiny human beings wearing fur coats.

I get a lot of emails asking: "Sarah, can I share my dinner with Buster?" The answer is usually no. Here is a list of things you absolutely should not be feeding your dog.

1. Jalapeño Poppers

Dogs do not understand the concept of Mexican food night. While a jalapeño popper might be a great snack for you while watching the game, your dog's stomach will not agree. They do not enjoy spicy things. Please keep the poppers to yourself.

2. Gravel and Small Rocks

This might sound obvious, but you'd be surprised. If you are landscaping your garden, do not mix gravel into your dog's bowl thinking it will "help with digestion like a bird." Dogs are not birds. They do not need rocks.

3. Loose Change

Quarters, dimes, pennies... none of these belong in a dog. Pennies do not have vitamins. If you drop a handful of coins on the floor, pick them up! Your dog does not need financial savings inside their stomach.

4. Rubber Bands

They might look like fun, chewy noodles, but they are not noodles. Stop letting your dog eat your office supplies.

Remember guys, a healthy dog is a happy dog! Stick to high-quality kibble and occasional safe treats like carrots. Leave the coins and rocks outside!
